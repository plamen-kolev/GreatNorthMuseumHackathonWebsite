About The Challenge

Over the course of two days, we will bring together 50 community members whose experiences and expertise could include academic studies in computing science, design, fine art, or museum studies, as well as practitioners, coders, makers, museum patrons and nature enthusiasts. Anyone is welcome – you don’t have to be a ‘hacker’ – you don’t even need to be ‘techie’, and you certainly won’t need to stay up all night, sleep on the floor, or exist off a diet of pizza and Coke. You just need an interest in and enthusiasm for reimagining and even remaking a beloved community space – Newcastle’s Great North Museum: Hancock. Your willingness to take part in a creative, inclusive design activity will feed into a process resulting in the development of a locally produced bespoke application for the GNM in 2017.

During the weekend we will help participants form interdisciplinary teams to compete in a Design Challenge, mentored by leading academics and practitioners from the worlds of museum

studies, human computer interaction, performance, and public interfaces. You will take part in a variety of activities designed to challenge preconceived notions of what a museum means to its community, with several keynote speakers selected provoke new ways of considering the museum experience.

By the end of the weekend your team will have created a prototype that will be judged by a panel of experts. Your team could win prizes and accolades, but most importantly, the prototypes will go forward as an inspiration for a further round of refinement in which regionally based software developers will be funded to implement an actual application for use at the GNM. By taking part and sharing your ideas, you have the opportunity to give back to your community, helping advance the state of the art in digital heritage at the Great North Museum, and making possible new and exciting ways to engage with the vast wealth of collections held by Tyne and Wear Archives and Museums.
